My whole life I have been taught to work for what I want out of life, whether it pertained to a long term goal or a simple task. Being only one of four daughters to a family of immigrants, I learned only on that nothing is given, sacrifices have to be made and hard work has to be put in to achieve my goals. My career goal is to become a teacher and I want to be able to teach students literature or spanish.

Being raised in a hispanic family, my first language is Spanish, meaning I am fluent in both english and spanish. I also have experience working with younger children, both professionally and personally. Having a large, extended family, it becomes routine to be around a lot kids. I learned pretty early on about the amount of work and energy needed to be able to care for them. Even now, I care for my 2 year old niece five days of the week. Volunteering at an after-school program also taught me a lot about work with kids. It taught me that patience is important to have with kids, especially for some that have more of a difficulty learning compared to other kids.
